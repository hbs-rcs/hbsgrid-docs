
# Other Research Computing Environments

## FASRC Cannon cluster

HBS personnel are able to use the resources at Faculty of Arts and
Sciences Research Computing (FASRC) to conduct their research. These
include access to larger storage pools and compute resources than are
available at HBS. There are costs associate with using these resources.
Please [contact RCS](mailto:research@hbs.edu) if you have questions or
for a consultation before signing on for a FASRC account and resources.

If you already have a FASRC account and need help, please see their
[support page](https://docs.rc.fas.harvard.edu/kb/support/),
[FAQs](https://docs.rc.fas.harvard.edu/kb/category/faqs/), or request
help via [email](mailto:rchelp@rc.fas.harvard.edu) or their [RC Ticket
page](https://portal.rc.fas.harvard.edu/login/?next=/rcrt/submit_ticket)
(login required).

## XSEDE

The [Extreme Science and Engineering Discovery Environment
(XSEDE)](https://www.xsede.org/home) is the most advanced, powerful, and
robust collection of integrated advanced digital resources and services
in the world. It is a single virtual system that scientists can use to
interactively share computing resources, data, and expertise.

In 2013, Harvard\'s FAS Research Computing joined the XSEDE project as
a [Campus Champion](https://www.xsede.org/campus-champions). In this
role, Research Computing acts as a local source of knowledge about
high-performance and high-throughput computing and other digital
services, opportunities and resources available through XSEDE and other
computing facilities.

HBS faculty are able to take advantage of the advanced computing
resources that are available as a part of the XSEDE community. Please
contact [Bob Freeman](mailto:rfreeman@hbs.edu) if you are interested in
or have questions about using XSEDE resources.


## Amazon Web Services (AWS)

HBS personnel can use AWS to conduct their research. AWS provides a
number of services for its customers, including 'bare metal',
unconfigured virtual machines, machine images with preset RAM, CPU, and
operating system configurations, machine images pre-configured with
software packages, and database hosting.

We advise you to please [contact RCS](mailto:research@hbs.edu) if you
have questions or for a consultation before signing on to AWS resources.
As AWS can be costly, this consultation will ensure that AWS is the
appropriate solution for your research needs.
