Introducing HBS Grid Application Launchers and Utilities

Harvard Business School (HBS) runs a computing cluster called the "HBS Grid". As of March 2021 this cluster consisted of 15 active compute nodes providing 620 CPUs, about 9 terabytes of memory, and about 200 terabytes of disk storage. The system is also equipped with high-end GPUs, making it useful for modern deep learning applications.

HBS Research Computing Services (RCS) is building powerful and user-friendly tools to bring the power of the HBS Grid to a wider community of HBS faculty, students, and staff. The video below serves as a visual introduction to our work.
